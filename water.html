Metallicity Gradients in DIFA (Navile) Water Sources

Published 2019 October 25

ABSTRACT

We present observations of metallicity in samples of water collected in our department. Estimates of a restricted group of people were taken into consideration by our team. Unfortunately, these measurements are heavily affected by instrumental (taste receptor cells) errors, so a conclusion about metallicity gradients cannot be drawn. However, it is clear that these metallicity values are extremely high. This could cause the onset of a disease known in Italian as problemi di calcoli (i.e. troubles with calculus 1 and 2), extremely harmful to students occupying this area. Many researchers are struggling to find some peculiar objects with zero-metallicity, which are thought to be water dispensers with a filtration system capable to zap some of the heavy elements. The community should now strive to bring here at least one water dispenser.
